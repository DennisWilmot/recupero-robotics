## Design Context

### Users
- **Primary**: VCs and investors doing due diligence — browsing during business hours on desktop, scanning for credibility signals (UPenn, NIH, patents, clinical trials), deciding in 90 seconds whether to take a meeting
- **Secondary**: Hospital administrators and rehabilitation clinic directors evaluating rehab technology purchases — looking for clinical evidence, cost justification, integration ease
- **Tertiary**: Clinicians/therapists, research partners (UPenn, Columbia), and stroke patients/caregivers seeking treatment options
- **Context**: Professional evaluation during daytime, on desktop primarily. This is a trust decision — they're deciding whether to invest money or adopt medical technology

### Brand Personality
- **Three words**: Grounded, clinical, pioneering
- **Emotional goals**: Confidence (this technology works), trust (backed by real institutions), hope (rehabilitation outcomes improve)
- **What it is NOT**: A flashy tech startup. Not a consumer gadget brand. Not a Silicon Valley play. This is a medical company that happens to build sophisticated technology — the medicine comes first, the tech serves it
- **Origin marker**: Founded in Philadelphia, born out of UPenn's Rehabilitation Robotics Lab — the Philly identity is subtle origin context, not a brand badge

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Clean, clinical, warm — like the environmental design of a modern rehabilitation center. Think institutional confidence with human warmth. The signage in a new children's hospital wing, not a gaming company's landing page
- **Theme**: LIGHT. The audience uses this during business hours in professional settings. Light backgrounds with green and white dominance, dark text for readability. Green accents signal health/growth/vitality
- **Colors**: Green (brand primary) + white/cream surfaces + dark navy text. Blues for trust/clinical elements. No neon, no glowing accents on dark, no tech-startup gradients
- **Anti-references**: Dark-mode SaaS dashboards, gaming company websites, generic GoDaddy builder templates, anything with cyan-on-dark or purple gradients
- **References**: Intuitive Surgical (institutional trust), Medtronic (medical credibility), but warmer and more human — this is rehabilitation, not surgical tools

### Design Principles
1. **Medicine first, technology second** — every design choice should feel like it came from a healthcare company that builds tech, not a tech company dabbling in medicine
2. **Credibility through restraint** — UPenn, NIH, patents, and clinical trials are the strongest signals. Let them speak without shouting. Clean presentation > flashy treatment
3. **Story through scroll** — the homepage is a narrative: problem → mission → solution → evidence → people → action. Each section advances the story
4. **Warmth in precision** — rehabilitation is deeply human work. The design should feel precise and trustworthy, but never cold. Real patient quotes, human photography, warm type
5. **Earned authority** — 8 IEEE publications, a 2.5-year clinical trial, and a US patent don't need decoration. Present them plainly and let the substance do the work
